The spotted jelly, lagoon jelly, golden medusa, or Papuan jellyfish (Mastigias papua) is a species of jellyfish from the Indo-Pacific. Instead of one single mouth, they have several smaller mouth openings in their oral arms, which are used to feed on zooplankton.
They have a lifespan of approximately 4 months and are active primarily in mid-summer to early autumn.

Numerous jellyfish swim as if floating in tanks of all sizes to create a spectacle that transports visitors from ordinary daily life into an extraordinary world.Jellyfish are believed to have remained unchanged for the past several 100 million years, with a beautiful, other-worldly appearance evoking memories of the distant aquatic past..

LAB

AQUA LAB

At the AQUA LAB, visitors can view the behind-the-scenes "backyard" operations of the aquarium, including aquarium keeping facilities and aquarium keepers tending to the needs of aquatic life.Here, visitors can observe each stage of the growth process of jellyfish from infancy to adulthood. Explanations of the behavior of jellyfish and sea anemone are also provided.The LAB offers visitors deeper insight into the biotope of these sorts of intriguing marine life.
